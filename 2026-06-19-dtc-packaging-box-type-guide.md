## The DTC Founder's Guide to Packaging Box Types

Picking the wrong box = 30% wasted budget. Here's how to choose.

### Tuck End Box
- **Best for**: E-commerce, beauty, daily goods
- **Why**: Simplest structure, lowest cost, automation-friendly
- **Size range**: 50×50×20mm ~ 400×300×100mm

### Mailer Box (Flip Top)
- **Best for**: Subscription boxes, gifts, DTC brands
- **Why**: Premium unboxing experience, strong brand feel, ships flat
- **Size range**: 80×80×30mm ~ 350×250×80mm

### Snap Lock Bottom Box
- **Best for**: Food, beauty, electronics accessories
- **Why**: Self-locking bottom, good weight support, no tape needed
- **Size range**: 40×40×40mm ~ 250×200×150mm

### Lid & Base Box (Rigid Box)
- **Best for**: Luxury goods, premium gifts
- **Why**: Maximum unboxing ritual, sturdy structure, reusable
- **Size range**: 60×60×30mm ~ 400×300×120mm

### Drawer Box (Slide Box)
- **Best for**: Premium gifts, lifestyle, electronics
- **Why**: Elegant pull-out mechanism, display-ready, refined feel
- **Size range**: 80×60×30mm ~ 300×200×80mm

### Quick Decision Matrix

| Scenario | Recommended Box | Why |
|----------|----------------|-----|
| E-commerce shipping | Tuck End / Mailer | Low cost, shock-resistant |
| Subscription box | Mailer | Premium unboxing |
| Food & beverage | Snap Lock Bottom | Food-safe |
| Luxury gift | Lid & Base / Drawer | Ritual & reuse |
| Prototyping | Tuck End | Cheapest single-unit option |
