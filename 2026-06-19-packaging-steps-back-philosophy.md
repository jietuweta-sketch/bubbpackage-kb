## Packaging Steps Back, Products Step Forward

### The Modern "Jewel Box" Problem

2,000 years ago, Han Feizi told a parable: a merchant sold a pearl in an ornate box — carved magnolia wood, scented with spices, inlaid with jade. The buyer took the box and returned the pearl.

Today, the joke is our daily reality:
- A lipstick in a fist-sized box with 3x the product weight in filler
- Mooncake tins nested like Russian dolls — 6 cakes, 8 layers
- E-commerce parcels wrapped in enough tape to circle the Earth

**400 million tons** of packaging waste annually. Half from over-packaging.

### bubbpack's Answer: Let Packaging Step Back

This isn't a slogan — it's a complete design philosophy:

1. **Practical**: Science-based protection. Nothing extra. No over-engineering.
2. **Simple**: Strip the unnecessary. Clean design gives the product room to speak.
3. **Sustainable**: Own the end-of-life. Mono-material. Plant-based. Fully recyclable.

### Why Sustainable Packaging Costs Less

The logic is simple: **less material = lower cost**.

Traditional over-packaging:
- Mixed materials (paper + plastic + metal) → unrecyclable → disposal cost
- Multi-layer structure → more material → higher cost
- Oversized → higher shipping

Minimalist packaging:
- Mono-material → 100% recyclable
- Reduced structure → 20%+ material savings
- Right-sized → lower logistics cost

### The Standard for Great Packaging

> When a customer opens the box, they should feel the product's value — not the packaging's opulence.

That's the ultimate mission: **protect the product, then gracefully exit**.
